Big Ben

Big Ben the 320 foot high Clock Tower is named after the largest bell, weighing over 13 tons, and was cast in 1858 at the Whitechapel Bell Foundry in East London. To this day one of the largest bells they have ever cast. Each clock face is over 7m in diameter.


Madame

London Marathon

The London Marathon is a long-distance running event held in London, United Kingdom, part of the World Marathon Majors. The event was first run on 29 March 1981 and has been held in the spring of every year since.


Love Beatless

The Beatles London Walking Tour is a must for any music fan! This tour is an incredible way to get acquainted with the beautiful city of London while also learning about one of the biggest pop bands in history.


Sherlock Holmes Museum

The world-famous consulting detective Sherlock Holmes lived at 221b Baker Street between 1881-1904, according to the stories written by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. Mr Holmes shared his rooms with his loyal friend and colleague Dr Watson.


Theme Parks

Thorpe Park, styled THORPE PARK Resort, is a theme park with a hotel in between the towns of Chertsey and Staines-upon-Thames, located within the area of Surrey, Greater London, UK. It is operated and owned by Merlin Entertainments


Changing of Guards

Buckingham Palace is the London residence and administrative headquarters of the reigning monarch of the United Kingdom. Located in the City of Westminster, the palace is often at the centre of state occasions and royal hospitality.


Fun for Kids

TOWER BRIDGE

London tower bridge is one of London’s famous bridges and one of many must-see landmarks in London with a glass floor and modern exhibitions. You can also see it from inside at the Tower Bridge Exhibition centre.


WEMBLEY STADIUM

The official home of the national stadium. Wembley Stadium is a football stadium in Wembley, London, England, which opened in 2007, on the site of the original Wembley Stadium, which was demolished from 2002–2003.


SHAKESPEARE GLOBE

Shakespeare’s Globe is the complex housing a reconstruction of the Globe Theatre, an Elizabethan playhouse associated with William Shakespeare, in the London Borough of Southwark, on the south bank of the River Thames.


A DAY OUTSIDE LONDON

Enjoy luxury shopping at Bicester Village, home to designer fashion brands with up to 60% off. From 46 minutes from London by train, plan your visit today! Discover a world of luxury at Bicester Village, the region’s ultimate shopping destination.


INCREDIBLE MUSEUM

A museum of the world, for the world. Discover over two million years of human history and culture. Some of the world-famous objects include the Rosetta Stone, the Parthenon sculptures and Egyptian mummies.


THE AMAZING NATIONAL GALLERY

The National Gallery houses the national collection of paintings in the Western European tradition from the 13th to the 19th centuries. It is on show 361 days a year, free of charge.


WIMBLEDON

The Championships, Wimbledon, commonly known simply as Wimbledon, is the oldest tennis tournament in the world, and is widely considered the most prestigious. It has been held at the All England Club in Wimbledon, London, since 1877.


CHELSEA FLOWER SHOW

Love gardening? Fanatical about flowers? Then the RHS Chelsea Flower Show should definitely be top of your London list! Held in the gorgeous grounds of the prestigious Royal Hospital Chelsea each May, Britain’s largest flower show has been running for over 100 years.
